Who’s afraid of the Form 1040?

The vast majority of Americans keep their distance from the 1040. At least 84 percent of us don’t even do our own taxes, instead relying on accountants, tax preparers, and software programs to figure out what we owe or what we’re getting back. But really, there’s little to fear. For most of us, our taxes are fairly straightforward—we report our income, take a standard deduction, and either pay what we owe or wait for our refund to arrive. It might not seem like it, but the process was designed to be foolproof. Simply follow the instructions, do a little math, and you’re set. But when you’re staring down 79 lines that ask about mysterious things like the alternative minimum tax or whether you paid tuition and fees or what your capital gains are, it’s easy to feel a little intimidated. Here, experts from the Tax Policy Center give you the information you need to help conquer your fears. Follow along as they break down some important sections of the most-common and most-dreaded income tax form.
